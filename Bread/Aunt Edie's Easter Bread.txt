Braided Easter Bread: Aunt Edie's Recipe

Ingredients:
2 tbsp yeast (4 packets)
1/2 cup lukewarm water
1 tbsp sugar
3/4 cup flour
1/2 cup shortening
1/2 cup sugar
1 tsp salt
1 egg white plus 1 egg (save the yolk!)
2 tsp lemon zest
2 tbsp lemon juice
2 1/4 cup flour

1. Sprinkle the 2 tbsp yeast onto the 1/2 cup water in a medium sized bowl. Let stand for 5 minutes. 
2. Stir in 1tbsp sugar and 3/4 cup flour until smooth. Cover and set aside for 1 hour in a warm place until doubled in size
3. Cream 1/2 cup shortening, 1/2 cup sugar, and 1 tsp salt with hand mixer. Add 1 egg and 1 egg white. Mix until smooth
4. Add yeast mixture, the lemon zest, 2 tbsp lemon juice, and 2 1/4 cup of flour until a smooth dough is formed. When the dough has pulled together, turn it out onto a lightly floured surface. Knead for 8 minutes
5. Lightly oil a large bowl. Place the dough in the bowl and turn to coat the dough with oil in the bowl. Cover with a damp cloth and let rise until doubled (about 1 hour)
6. Deflate the dough and turn it out onto a lightly floured surface
7. Divide dough in 2. roll out to 2-18 inch ropes. Grease baking sheet. Braid the two ropes, leaving room for 3 colored uncooked eggs to fit inside the ropes. 
8. Coat the bread with egg yolk mixed with some water (avoid going near the eggs, the color will bleed). Bake for 40 minutes on 350 degrees
