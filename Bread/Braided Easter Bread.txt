6 to 6. cups of all purpose flour
1/2 cup sugar
2 packages of active dry yeast
1 to 2 tsp ground cardamom
1 tsp salt
1 1/2 cups milk
6 tbsp butter, cubed
4 eggs
3 to 6 hard cooked eggs
vegetable oil
2 tbsp cold water

1) in a large bowl, combine 2 cups flour, sugar, yeast, cardamom, and salt. in a suacepan, heat milk and butter to 120-130 degrees. add to dry ingredients; beat just until moistened. add 3 eggs; beat until smooth. stir in enough remianing flour to form a soft dough
2) turn onto a floured surface; knead until smooth and elastic, about 6-8 minutes. place in a greased bowl, turning once to grease top. cover and let rise in a warm place until doubled, about 45 minutes
3) dye hard boiled eggs; lightly rub withoil. punch dough down. turn onto a lgihtly floured surface; divide dough into thirds. shape each portion into a 24 inch rope
4) place ropes on a greased baking sheet and braid; bring ends together to form a ring. pinch ends to seal. gently separate braided ropes and tuck dyed eggs into openings. cover and let rise until doubled, about 20 minutes
5) beat water and remaining egg; gently brush over dough. bake at 375 for 28 to 32 minutes or until golden brown. remove from pan to a wire rack and cool. refrigerate leftovers
