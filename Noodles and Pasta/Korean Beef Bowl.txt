0.25 lb lean ground beef
1/16 onion, chopped
0.75 garlic cloves, minced
0.50 teaspoon freshly grated ginger
1 cup cooked rice/quinoa, sprialized veggies
Korean Sauce
1/12 cup low sodium soy sauce
0.50 tablespoon brown sugar (See Note)
0.50 tablespoon Chinese rice wine may substitute dry sherry
0.50 tablespoon Gochujang or more if desired*
0.50 teaspoon sesame oil
1/16 teaspoon pepper
0.50 teaspoon cornstarch
Stir Fry
0.25 teaspoon olive oil
0.25 large zucchini, sliced
0.25 red bell pepper, chopped
0.25 carrot, thinly sliced

Garnish
green onions
chili paste
sesame seeds


INSTRUCTIONS
Whisk together Korean Sauce in a medium bowl. Set aside.
Brown beef and onion in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add garlic and ginger and sauté an additional 30 seconds. Drain off any excess fat.
Add Korean Sauce and bring to a boil. Reduce to a simmer for 5-10 minutes or until reduced and thickened. Taste and add chili paste for spicier, brown sugar for sweeter. Remove from skillet but do not wipe out.
Add one teaspoon olive oil and heat over medium-high heat. Add zucchini, carrots and red bell peppers (or other desired vegetables) and stir fry 2-3 minutes or to your desired crisp-tenderness.
Layer 4 bowls with rice/quinoa and top with Korean beef and vegetables.
