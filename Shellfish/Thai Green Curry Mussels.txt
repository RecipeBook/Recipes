Thai Green Curry Mussels (almost the same as Thai mussels)

Ingredients:
8 sprigs cilantro (stems and leaves), roughly chopped and separated
4 medium cloves of garlic, thinly sliced
2 small shallots, thinly sliced
1/2 tsp coriander seeds
1/2 tsp red chili flakes
1 tsp zest plus 1 tbsp lime juice from 1 lime
kosher salt
1 (15oz) can of coconut milk/cream (do not shake!)
1 tbsp vegetable/canola oil
1 tbsp thai green curry paste
1 tbsp fish sauce (more to taste)
1 tbsp brown sugar
2 pounds mussels
1 small thai or serranno chilli, thinly sliced (optional)

Direction:
1. combine cilantro stems, half of garlic, half of shallot, coriander seeds, pepper flakes, lime zest, and pinch of kosher salt in a mortar and pestle. pound and grind until a smooth paste is formed
2. scoop 2 tbsp of thick cream from the top of the can of coconut milk and add to large saucepan. add oil and heat over medium heat until shimmering and sputtering. 
3. add remaining garlic and shallots, pounded paste, and the store-bought curry paste. cook, stirring and scraping the paste around the bottom of the pan until very aromatic, about 4 minutes
4. add remaining contents of coconut milk can, sugar, and fish sauce. bring to a simmer and cook until rich and aromatic, about 3 minutes. season to taste with more fish sauce as desired
5. add mussels, stir, cover, and cook SHAKE PAN CONSTANTLY and peeking every 30 seconds. as soon as all mussels are open, stir in chopped cilantro leaves, fresh chili (optional), and lime juice. serve immediately with addtional limes at the table
